Og Mandino Quotes - Page 4

Cherish each hour of this day for it can NEVER return.

If I must be a slave to habit let me be a slave to good habits.

There is an immeasurable distance between late and too late.

My days of whining and complaining about others have come to an end. Nothing is easier than fault finding. All it will do is discolor my personality so that none will want to associate with me. That was my old life. No more.

Always will I dig for reasons to applaud; never will I scratch for excuses to gossip. When I am tempted to criticize I will bite on my tongue; when I am moved to praise I will shout from the roofs.

Only a habit can subdue another habit.

I will greet this day with love in my heart.

I will laugh at the world. And most of all, I will laugh at myself for man is most comical when he takes himself too seriously.

First, recognize that you are not a sheep who will be satisfied with only a few nibbles of dry grass or with following the herd as they wander aimlessly, bleating and whining, all of their days. Separate yourself now from the multitude of humanity so that you will be able to control your own destiny. Remember that what others think and say and do need never influence what you think and say and do.

Realize how good you really are.

Always reward your long hours of labor and toil in the very best way, surrounded by your family. Nurture their love carefully, remembering that your children need models, not critics, and your own progress will hasten when you constantly strive to present your best side to your children. And even if you have failed at all else in the eyes of the world, if you have a loving family, you are a success.

You are not here to fritter away your precious hours when you have the ability to accomplish so much by making a slight change in your routine.

I will walk where failures fear to walk.

Each day will be triumphant only when your smiles bring forth smiles from others.

I will remove from my vocabulary such words and phrases as quit, cannot, unable, impossible, out of the question, improbable, failure, unworkable, hopeless, and retreat; for they are the words of fools.

Procrastination can be driven from your personality through the simple technique of constantly commanding yourself to get into action . . . and then obeying that command immediately.

Good habits are the key to all success. Bad habits are the unlocked door to failure.

Envy, like the worm, is always attracted to the fairest apple

I will waste not even a precious second today in anger or hate or jealousy or selfishness. I know that the seeds I sow I will harvest, because every action, good or bad, is always followed by an equal reaction. I will plant only good seeds this day.

Today I begin a new life. Today I shed my old skin which hath, too long, suffered the bruises of failure and the wounds of mediocrity. Today I am born anew and my birthplace is a vineyard where there is fruit for all.

Take great comfort in knowing that ALL great feats are accomplished one small step at a time.

He who worries about calamities suffers them twice over.

I am natures greatest miracle. Since the beginning of time never has there been another with my heart, my eyes, my ears, my hands, my hair, my mouth. None that came before, none that live today, and none that come tomorrow can walk and talk and move and think exactly like me. I am a unique creature.

Use wisely your power of choice.

Act now, for now is all you have.
