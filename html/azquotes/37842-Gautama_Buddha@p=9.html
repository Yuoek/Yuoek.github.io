Gautama Buddha Quotes - Page 9

In the same way that rain breaks into a house with a bad roof, desire breaks into the mind that has not been practising meditation.

All living beings have Buddha nature and can become Buddhas.

He who experiences the unity of life sees his own self in all beings.

Those who recognize the existence of suffering, its cause, its remedy, and its cessation, have fathomed the four noble truths. They will walk in the right path.

Radiate boundless love towards the entire world - above, below, and across - unhindered, without ill will, without enmity.

A person is not learned nor wise because he talks much; the person who is patient, free from hatred and fear, that person is called learned and wise.

Remembering a wrong is like carrying a burden on the mind.

To walk safely through the maze of human life, one needs the light of wisdom and the guidance of virtue.

There is no meditation without wisdom, and there is no wisdom without meditation. When a man has both meditation and wisdom, he is indeed close to nirvana.

One who previously made bad karma, but who reforms and creates good karma, brightens the world like the moon appearing from behind a cloud.

To awaken, sit calmly, letting each breath clear your mind and open your heart.

Drink deeply. Live in serenity and joy.

Mindfulness of the body leads to nirvana.

The ignorant man is an ox. He grows in size, not in wisdom.

Doubt everything. Find your own light.

You, as much as anyone in the universe, deserve your love and respect.

We are the same as plants, as trees, as other people, as the rain that falls. We consist of that which is around us, we are the same as everything.

Those who have failed to work toward the truth have missed the purpose of living.

The fool who thinks he is wise is just a fool. The fool who knows he is a fool is wise indeed.

Nothing is to be clung to as I, me or mine

It is the fool who is haunted by fears, dread of dangers, oppression of mind, not the wise man.

From the passions arise worry, and from worry arises fear. Away with the passions, and no fear, no worry.

Whatever is subject to origination is all subject to cessation.

When we feel happy and peaceful, our happiness and peace radiates around us, and others can enjoy it as well. This is called 'the enjoyment of others of our body of bliss'.

In protecting oneself, others are protected; In protecting others, oneself is protected.
