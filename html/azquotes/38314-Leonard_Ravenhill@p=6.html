Leonard Ravenhill Quotes - Page 6

The Bible definitely is infallible, how else could it survive so many years of bad preaching?

A true shepherd leads the way. He does not merely point the way.

I read of the revivals of the past, great sweeping revivals where thousands of men were swept into the Kingdom of God. I read about Charles G. Finney winning his thousands and his hundreds of thousands of souls to Christ. Then I picked up a book and read the messages of Charles G. Finney and the message of Jonathan Edwards on 'Sinners in the Hands of an Angry God,' and I said, 'No wonder men trembled; no wonder they fell in the altars and cried out in repentance and sobbed their way to the throne of grace!'

Books on prayer are good, but not good enough. As books on cooking are good but hopeless unless there is food to work on, so with prayer. One can read a library of prayer books and not be one whit more powerful in prayer. We must learn to pray, and we must pray to learn to pray.

If we had more hell fire preaching in the pulpit then we would have less hell bound people in the pew.

The more men pray, the less worldly they become. The less they pray, the more worldly they become. I am, of course, speaking of professing Christians at this point.

Preaching affects men; prayer affects God. Preaching affects time; prayer affects eternity

Can any deny that in the modern church setup the main cause of anxiety is money? Yet that which tries the modern churches the most, troubled the New Testament Church the least. Our emphasis is on paying; theirs was on praying. When we have paid, the place is taken; when they had prayed, the place was shaken!

What are you going to do when you get to eternity, if you can't stick in an hour with God down here?

A wet eyed preacher will never preach dry sermons

Do the Pentecostals look back with shame as they remember when they dwelt across the theological tracks, but with the glory of the Lord in their midst? When they had a normal church life, which meant nights of prayers, followed by signs and wonders, and diverse miracles, and genuine gifts of the Holy Ghost? When they were not clock watchers, and their meetings lasted for hours, saturated with holy power? Have we no tears for these memories, or shame that our children know nothing of such power?

You can't develop character by reading books. You develop it from conflict.

When you're born in fire, smoke will never do.

Men build our churches but do not enter them, print our Bibles but do not read them, talk about God but do not believe Him, speak of Christ but do not trust Him for salvation, sing our hymns and then forget them.

Three things faith does: it reckons on God; it risks with God (and) it rests in God.

The tragedy in our colleges and seminaries right now is that we turn men out who know the word of God. That is never going to turn the world.
The question is not whether they know the Word of God....
The question is......Do they know the God of the Word?

Our God is a consuming fire. He consumes pride, lust, materialism, and other sin.

Better for you to have one sleepless night on earth than millions in hell.

The surest thing in the world is not death and taxes, it's death and eternity. Yet, we're so unconcerned.

Easy believeism dishonors the blood and prostitutes the altar. We must alter the altar, for the altar is a place to die on. Let those who will not pay this price leave it alone!

The last words of Jesus to the church (in Revelation) were ‘Repent!’

A man with an experience of God is never at the mercy of a man with an argument.

The only vision (many) of you have is television.

Today Christians spend more money on dog food then missions

Isn't it amazing that God gives breath to a man who is going to blaspheme Him all day!
