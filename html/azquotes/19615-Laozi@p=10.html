Laozi Quotes - Page 10

Do you think that you can clear your mind by sitting constantly in silent meditation? This makes your mind narrow, not clear. Integral awareness is fluid and adaptable, present in all places and at all times. That is true meditation. ... The Tao is clear and simple, and it doesn't avoid the world.

The world is a sacred vessel that cannot be changed. He who changes it will destroy it. He who seizes it will lose it.

So the unwanting soul sees what's hidden, and the ever-wanting soul sees only what it wants.

What man has more than enough and gives it to the world? Only the man of Tao.

Yet mystery and reality emerge from the same source.

Pride attaches undue importance to the superiority of one's status in the eyes of others; and shame is fear of humiliation at one's inferior status in the estimation of others. When one sets one's heart on being highly esteemed, and achieves such rating, then he or she is automatically involved in fear of losing status.

All things issue from it; all things return to it. To find the origin, trace back the manifestations. When you recognize the children and find the mother, you will be free of sorrow. If you close your mind in judgements and traffic with desires, your heart will be troubled. If you keep your mind from judging and aren't led by the senses, your heart will find peace. Seeing into darkness is clarity. Knowing how to yield is strength. Use your own light and return to the source of light. This is called practicing eternity.

When you let go of what you are, you become what you might be.

. . . Intelligence is not the same thing as wisdom.

The usefulness of a pot comes from its emptiness.

As long as we have hope, we have direction, the energy to move, and the map to move by. We have a hundred alternatives, a thousand paths and an infinity of dreams. Hopeful, we are halfway to where we want to go; hopeless, we are lost forever.

Become totally empty
Quiet the restlessness of the mind
Only then will you witness everything unfolding from emptiness

Always without desire we must be found, If its deep mystery we would sound; But if desire always within us be, Its outer fringe is all that we shall see.

Only he who does not seek to be ahead of others is capable of living in harmony with everyone.

Excellent warriors are not violent.

. . . These are notions of the mind, which is like a knife, always chipping away at the Tao, trying to render it graspable and manageable. But that which is beyond form is ungraspable, and that which is beyond knowing is unmanageable. There is, however, this consolation: She who lets go of the knife will find the Tao at her fingertips.

Simplicity, patience, compassion.
These three are your greatest treasures.
Simple in actions and thoughts, you return to the source of being.
Patient with both friends and enemies, you accord with the way things are.
Compassionate toward yourself, you reconcile all beings in the world.

The world is won by those who let it go.

Throw away sacredness and wisdom and people will be one hundred times happier.

The mystical techniques for achieving immortality are revealed only to those who have dissolved all ties to the gross worldly realm of duality, conflict, and dogma. As long as your shallow worldly ambitions exist, the door will not open.

If you go searching for the Great Creator, you will come back empty-handed. The source of the universe is ultimately unknowable, a great invisible river flowing forever through a vast and fertile valley. Silent and uncreated, it creates all things.

Quarrel with a friend - and you are both wrong.

The best way to manage anything is by making use of its own nature.

Knowledge of the eternal is all-embracing. To be all-embracing leads to righteousness, which is majestic.

The heavy is the root of the light. The tranquil is the ruler of the hasty.
