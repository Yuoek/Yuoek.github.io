Ralph Waldo Emerson Quotes - Page 6

Meet your failure nobly, and it will not differ from success.

Many eyes go through the meadow, but few see the flowers in it

The world is plentiful with honey, but only the humble bee can collect it.

When it is dark enough, men see the stars.

Finish each day and be done with it. You have done what you could. Learn from it... tomorrow is a new day.

Stay at home in your mind. Don't recite other people's opinions.

Trust your instinct to the end, though you can render no reason.

What is the hardest task in the world? To think.

People are very inclined to set moral standards for others.

The task ahead of us is never as great as the power behind us.

The music that can deepest reach and cure all ill is cordial speech.

We boil at different degrees.

The reason why the world lacks unity, and lies broken and in heaps, is, because man is disunited with himself.

Thought is the seed of action.

Men are what their mothers made them.

Whatever you do, you need courage. Whatever course you decide upon, there is always someone to tell you that you are wrong.

Perception is a mirror not a fact. And what I look on is my state of mind, reflected outward.

The wise man in the storm prays God not for safety from danger but for deliverance from fear. It is the storm within which endangers him[,] not the storm without.

It is one of the beautiful compensations in this life that no one can sincerely try to help another without helping himself.

To different minds, the same world is a hell, and a heaven.

A great man stands on God. A small man on a great man.

Without ambition one starts nothing. Without work one finishes nothing. The prize will not be sent to you. You have to win it. The man who knows how will always have a job. The man who also knows why will always be his boss. As to methods there may be a million and then some, but principles are few. The man who grasps principles can successfully select his own methods. The man who tries methods, ignoring principles, is sure to have trouble.

We must be our own before we can be another's.

Patience and fortitude conquer all things.

What you are comes to you.
