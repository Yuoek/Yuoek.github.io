When it comes to Fashion Week, I think it's time to hit a refresh.

I’m so thrilled to manufacture in LA and to support the finely skilled craftsmen and women. Making it in America ensures that the fashion industry will continue to thrive in this country for years to come.

It's so easy for me to get caught up in the feeling of a city like Venice, where everything is just beautiful color and gorgeous buildings that are so peaceful. You can roam around and get lost in the labyrinth.

I really love to resurrect pieces from my past collections and wear them to fashion week parties.

I think I would like to have lived in the 1930s and worn beautiful bias cut dresses all the time.

The Thirties are a great mix of everyday glamour and something a bit more practical.

I’ve always wanted to tackle the casual part of dressing. Knits to me are always just easy. I’ve fantasized about packing a suitcase of only knits: You just throw them in, roll them in a ball, pull them out and they still look fabulous.

It's just fun to be in Paris.
