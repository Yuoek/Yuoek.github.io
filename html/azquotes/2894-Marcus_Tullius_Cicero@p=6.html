Marcus Tullius Cicero Quotes - Page 6

I am never less alone than when alone.

Justice consists in doing no injury to men; decency in giving them no offense.

The absolute good is not a matter of opinion but of nature.

When time and need require, we should resist with all our might, and prefer death to slavery and disgrace.

Friends, though absent, are still present.

In this statement, my Scipio, I build on your own admirable definition, that there can be no community, properly so called, unless it be regulated by a combination of rights. And by this definition it appears that a multitude of men may be just as tyrannical as a single despot and indeed this is the most odious of all tyrannies, since no monster can be more barbarous than the mob, which assumes the name and mask of the people.

Civilized people are taught by logic, barbarians, by necessity, communities by tradition; and the lesson inculcated even in wild beasts by nature itself. They learn that they have to defend their own bodies and persons lives from violence of any and every kind by all means within their power.

The life of the dead is placed in the memory of the living.

If some lose their whole fortunes, they will drag many more down with them . . . believe me that the whole system of credit and finance which is carried on here at Rome in the Forum, is inextricably bound up with the revenues of the Asiatic province. If Those revenues are destroyed, our whole system of credit will come down with a crash.

I prefer the wisdom of the uneducated to the folly of the loquacious.

What is permissible is not always honorable.

Knowledge which is divorced from justice, may be called cunning rather than wisdom.

Of all nature's gifts to the human race, what is sweeter to a man than his children?

Wisdom often exists under a shabby coat.

Time obliterates the fictions of opinion and confirms the decisions of nature.

I hear Socrates saying that the best seasoning for food is hunger; for drink, thirst.

The mere act of believing that some wrongful course of action constitutes an advantage is pernicious.

Probability is the very guide of life.

Justice extorts no reward, no kind of price; she is sought, therefore, for her own sake.

The life given us, by nature is short; but the memory of a well-spent life is eternal.

A tear dries quickly when it is shed for troubles of others.

It is difficult to persuade mankind that the love of virtue is the love of themselves.

A man's own manner and character is what most becomes him.

The purpose of education is to free the student from the tyranny of the present.

Genius is fostered by industry.
