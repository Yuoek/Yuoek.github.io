Philip Larkin Quotes - Page 6

On me your voice falls as they say love should, Like an enormous yes.

I have started to say
"A quarter of a century"
Or "thirty years back"
About my own life.

My age fallen away like white swaddling
Floats in the middle distance, becomes
An inhabited cloud.

Joy
Is for the simple or the great to feel,
Neither of which we are.

But superstition, like belief, must die.

I never think of poetry or the poetry scene, only separate poems written by individuals.

Any memory for the most part depending on chance.

And the case of butterflies so rich it looks As if all summer settled there and died.
