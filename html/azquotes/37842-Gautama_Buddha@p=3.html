Gautama Buddha Quotes - Page 3

An outside enemy exists only if there is anger
inside.

The greatest impurity is ignorance. Free yourself from it. Be pure.

Even loss and betrayal can bring us awakening.

Peace comes from within; do not try to seek it without.

There is only one time when it is essential to awaken. That time is now.

Just as a snake sheds its skin, we must shed our past over and over again.

The whole secret of existence is to have no fear.

As a lotus flower is born in water, grows in water and rises out of water to stand above it unsoiled, so I, born in the world, raised in the world having overcome the world, live unsoiled by the world

Be vigilant; guard your mind against negative thoughts

Do not speak - unless it improves on silence.

Don't keep searching for the truth, just let go of your opinions.

Holding on to anger is like poisoning yourself and hoping someone else will die.

Develop a mind that is vast like space, where experiences both pleasant and unpleasant can appear and disappear without conflict, struggle or harm. Rest in a mind like vast sky.

Pain is certain, suffering is optional.

Always be mindful of the kindness and not the faults of others

Why cling to the pain and the wrongs of yesterday? Why hold on to the very things that keep you from hope and love.

Do not look for a sanctuary in anyone except your self.

Nirvana is this moment seen directly. There is no where else than here. The only gate is now. The only doorway is your own body and mind. There’s nowhere to go. There’s nothing else to be. There’s no destination. It’s not something to aim for in the afterlife. It’s simply the quality of this moment.

A fool learns nothing from a wise man; but a wise man learns from a fool.

The less you have, the less you have to worry about.

To utter pleasant words without practicing them, is like a fine flower without fragrance.

The thought manifests as the word. The word manifests as the deed. The deed develops into habit. And the habit hardens into character. So watch the thought and its ways with care. And let it spring from love, born out of concern for all beings.

There is nothing more dreadful than the habit of doubt. Doubt separates people. It is a poison that disintegrates friendships and breaks up pleasant.

Believe nothing because it is written in books. Believe nothing because wise men say it is so. Believe nothing because it is religious doctrine. Believe it only because you yourself know it to be true.

The more completely we give of ourselves, the more completely the world gives back to us.
