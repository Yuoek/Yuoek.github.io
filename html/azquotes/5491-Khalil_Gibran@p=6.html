Khalil Gibran Quotes - Page 6

Love is the only freedom in the world because it so elevates the spirit that the laws of humanity and the phenomena of nature do not alter its course.

Love has the power that dispels death; charm that conquers the enemy.

A friend who is far away is sometimes much nearer than one who is at hand. Is not the mountain far more awe-inspiring and more clearly visible to one passing through the valley than to those who inhabit the mountain?

I existed from all eternity and, behold, I am here; and I shall exist till the end of time, for my being has no end.

You may chain my hands, you may shackle my feet; you may even throw me into a dark prison; but you shall not enslave my thinking, because it is free!

Our pain carves out a larger space for love to fill.

No human relation gives one possession in another—every two souls are absolutely different. In friendship or in love, the two side by side raise hands together to find what one cannot reach alone.

We are all like the bright moon, we still have our darker side.

The sun teaches to all things that grow their longing for the light.
But it is night that raises them to the stars.

I have found both freedom and safety in my madness; the freedom of loneliness and the safety from being understood, for those who understand us enslave something in us.

You shall be held down by the chains of your own judgment.

God created music as a common language for all men. It inspires the poets, the composers and the architects. It lures us to search our souls for the meaning of the mysteries described in ancient books.

The deeper that sorrow carves into your being, the more joy you can contain.

I wash my hands of those who imagine chattering to be knowledge, silence to be ignorance, and affection to be art.

When you love you should not say, “God is in my heart,” but rather, “I am in the heart of God.

We wanderers, ever seeking the lonelier way, begin no day where we have ended another day; and no sunrise finds us where sunset left us.

They deem me mad because I will not sell my days for gold; and I deem them mad because they think my days have a price.

Rest in reason. Move in Passion.

Each day look into your conscience and amend your faults; if you fail in this duty you will be untrue to the Knowledge and Reason that are within you. Keep a watchful eye over yourself as if you were your own enemy; for you cannot learn to govern yourself, unless you first learn to govern your own passions and obey the dictates of your conscience.

When you are sorrowful look again in your heart, and you shall see that in truth you are weeping for that which has been your delight. Some of you say, "Joy is greater than sorrow," and others say, "Nay, sorrow is the greater." But I say unto you, they are inseparable. Together they come, and when one sits alone with you at your board, remember that the other is asleep upon your bed. Verily you are suspended like scales between your sorrow and your joy.

Strange that creatures without backbones have the hardest shells.

Coming generations will learn equality from poverty, and love from woes.

Are you a politician asking what your country can do for you or a zealous one asking what you can do for your country? If you are the first, then you are a parasite; if the second, then you are an oasis in the desert.

Oh heart, if one should say to you that the soul perishes like the body, answer that the flower withers, but the seed remains.

The tiny flame that lights up the human heart is like a blazing torch that comes down from heaven to light up the paths of mankind. For in one soul are contained the hopes and feelings of all Mankind.
