Edward Abbey Quotes - Page 2

Truth is always the enemy of power. And power the enemy of truth.

Anarchism is not a romantic fable but the hardheaded realization, based on five thousand years of experience, that we cannot entrust the management of our lives to kings, priests, politicians, generals, and county commissioners.

Those who fear death most are those who enjoy life least.

I have been called a curmudgeon, which my obsolescent dictionary defines as a "surly, illmannered, badtempered fellow." ... Nowadays, curmudgeon is likely to refer to anyone who hates hypocrisy, cant, sham, dogmatic ideologies, the pretenses and evasions of euphemism, and has the nerve to point out unpleasant facts and takes the trouble to impale these sins on the skewer of humor and roast them over the fires of empiric fact, common sense, and native intelligence. In this nation of bleating sheep and braying jackasses, it then becomes an honor to be labeled curmudgeon.

Life is too short for grief. Or regret. Or bullshit.

An empty man is full of himself.

The purpose of love, sex, and marriage is the production and raising of children. But look about you: Most people have no business having children. They are unqualified, either genetically or culturally or both, to reproduce such sorry specimens as themselves. Of all our privileges, the license to breed is the one most grossly abused.

The idea of wilderness needs no defense, it only needs defenders.

Better a cruel truth than a comfortable delusion.

It is not enough to understand the natural world; the point is to defend and
preserve it.

All governments need enemies. How else to justify their existence?

A patriot must always be ready to defend his country against his government.

How to Overthrow the System: brew your own beer; kick in your Tee Vee; kill your own beef; build your own cabin and piss off the front porch whenever you bloody well feel like it.

Power is always dangerous. Power attracts the worst and corrupts the best.

The real work of men was hunting meat. The invention of agriculture was a giant step in the wrong direction, leading to serfdom, cities, and empire. From a race of hunters, artists, warriors, and tamers of horses, we degraded ourselves to what we are now: clerks, functionaries, laborers, entertainers, processors of information.

Heaven is home. Utopia is here. Nirvana is now.

Recorded history is largely an account of the crimes and disasters committed by banal little men at the levers of imperial machines.

You can't study the darkness by flooding it with light.

Only the half-mad are wholly alive.

Liberty cannot be guaranteed by law. Nor by any thing else except the resolution of free citizens to defend their liberties.

The earth, like the sun, like the air, belongs to everyone - and to no one.

The earth is not a mechanism but an organism, a being with its own life and its own reasons, where the support and sustenance of the human animal is incidental. If man in his newfound power and vanity persists in the attempt to remake the planet in his own image, he will succeed only in destroying himself - not the planet. The earth will survive our most ingenious folly.

The most common form of terrorism in the U.S.A. is that carried on by bulldozers and chain saws.

I am not an atheist but an earthiest. Be true to the earth.

It is not death or dying that is tragic, but rather to have existed without fully participating in life- that is the deepest personal tragedy.
