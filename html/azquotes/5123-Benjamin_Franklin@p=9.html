Benjamin Franklin Quotes - Page 9

Clean your finger before you point at my spots.

He that is rich need not live sparingly,
and he that can live sparingly need not be rich.

Wish not so much to live long as to live well.

The rotten apple spoils his companion.

If you, do what you should not, you must bear what you would not.

I saw few die of hunger; of eating, a hundred thousand.

There are three things extremely hard: steel, a diamond, and to know one's self.

Content makes poor men rich; discontent makes rich men poor.

Among the numerous luxuries of the table...coffee may be considered as one of the most valuable. It excites cheerfulness without intoxication; and the pleasing flow of spirits which it occasions...is never followed by sadness, languor or debility.

Take a coin from your purse and invest it in your mind. It will come pouring out of your mind and overflow your purse.

Sloth makes all things difficult, but industry all things easy.

Sarcasm is the lowest form of humor but the highest form of flattery.

Those disputing, contradicting, and confuting people are generally unfortunate in their affairs. They get victory, sometimes, but they never get good will, which would be of more use to them.

By playing at Chess then, we may learn... First: Foresight. Second: Circumspection. Third: Caution.

You will discover 3 trustworthy mates, an aged wife, an aged canine, and ready dollars.

When you are in debt, then you are a slave.

He that composes himself is wiser than he that composes a book.

Beware of meat twice boiled, and an old foe reconciled.

How many observe Christ's birthday! How few, His precepts!

You will find the key to success under the alarm clock.

Write your injuries in dust, your benefits in marble.

A man must have a good deal of vanity who believes, and a good deal of boldness who affirms, that all the doctrines he holds are true, and all he rejects are false.

Don't judge men's wealth or godliness by their Sunday appearance.

We should make the poor uncomfortable and kick them out of poverty.

There are three sorts of people in the world: Those who are immovable, people who don't get it, or don't want to do anything about it; there are people who are movable, people who see the need for change and are prepared to listen to it; and there are people who move, people who make things happen.
