Epictetus Quotes - Page 9

It is difficulties that show what men are.

Truth is a thing immortal and perpetual, and it gives to us a beauty that fades not away in time, nor does it take away the freedom of speech which proceeds from justice; but it gives to us the knowledge of what is just and lawful, separating from them the unjust and refuting them.

It is no easy thing for a principle to become a man's own unless each day he maintains it and works it out in his life.

When we name things correctly, we comprehend them correctly, without adding information or judgements that aren't there. Does someone bathe quickly? Don't say be bathes poorly, but quickly. Name the situation as it is, don't filter it through your judgments. Give your assent only to that which is actually true.

If I can acquire money and also keep myself modest and faithful and magnanimous, point out the way, and I will acquire it.

It is not he who gives abuse that affronts, but the view that we take of it as insulting; so that when one provokes you it is your own opinion which is provoking.

Contentment, as it is a short road and pleasant, has great delight and little trouble.

Attach yourself to what is spiritually superior, regardless of what other people think or do. Hold to your true aspirations no matter what is going on around you.

If you can make music with someone you don't need words.
If you wish to be a writer, write.

The cause of all human evils is the not being able to apply general principles to special cases.

When you close your doors, and make darkness within, remember never to say that you are alone, for you are not alone; nay, God is within, and your genius is within. And what need have they of light to see what you are doing?

We should not have either a blunt knife or a freedom of speech which is ill-managed.

Difficulty shows what men are. Therefore when a difficulty falls upon you, remember that God, like a trainer of wrestlers, has matched you with a rough young man. Why? So that you may become an Olympic conqueror; but it is not accomplished without sweat.

Unless we place our religion and our treasure in the same thing, religion will always be sacrificed.

It is a mark of a mean capacity to spend much time on the things which concern the body, such as much exercise, much eating, much drinking, much easing of the body, much copulation. But these things should be done as subordinate things: and let all your care be directed to the mind.

It is better to die of hunger having lived without grief and fear, than to live with a troubled spirit, amid abundance

Be not diverted from your duty by any idle reflections the silly world may make upon you, for their censures are not in your power and should not be at all your concerns.

Do not strive for things occurring to occur as you wish, but wish the things occurring as they occur, and you will flow well.

If you seek truth you will not seek victory by dishonorable means, and if you find truth you will become invincible.

Covetousness like jealousy, when it has taken root, never leaves a person, but with their life. Cowardice is the dread of what will happen.

The wise realize that some things are within their control, and most things are not. They learn early on to distinguish between what they can and can't regulate.

Envy is the antagonist of the fortunate.

The soul is unwillingly deprived of truth.

We are not disturbed by what happens to us, but by our thoughts about what happens to us.

The foolish and the uneducated have little use for freedom. Only the educated are free.
