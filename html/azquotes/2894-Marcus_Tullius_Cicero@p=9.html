Marcus Tullius Cicero Quotes - Page 9

The multitude of fools is a protection to the wise.

Vivere est cogitare. (To think is to live)

Brevity is a great charm of eloquence.

Everything is alive... Everything is interconnected.

Memory is the treasury and guardian of all things.

A bachelor's bed is the most pleasant.

What is dishonorably got, is dishonorably squandered.

God's law is 'right reason.' When perfectly understood it is called 'wisdom.' When applied by government in regulating human relations it is called 'justice.

Never go to excess, but let moderation be your guide.

It is a crime to put a Roman citizen in chains, it is an enormity to flog one, sheer murder to slay one: what, then, shall I say of crucifixion? It is impossible to find the word for such an abomination.

We must be ever on the search for some persons whom we shall love and who will love us in return. If good will and affection are taken away, every joy is taken from life.

It is the character of a brave and resolute man not to be ruffled by adversity and not to desert his post.

Friends are proved by adversity.

We are motivated by a keen desire for praise, and the better a man is the more he is inspired by glory. The very philosophers themselves, even in those books which they write in contempt of glory, inscribe their names.

The administration of government, like a guardianship ought to be directed to the good of those who confer, not of those who receive the trust.

For what is there more hideous than avarice, more brutal than lust, more contemptible than cowardice, more base than stupidity and folly?

The first bond of society is marriage.

The good of the people is the greatest law.

Every man should bear his own grievances rather than detract from the comforts of another.

The more virtuous any man is, the less easily does he suspect others to be vicious.

Whatever befalls in accordance with nature should be accounted good.

The house should derive dignity from the master, not the master from the house.

In so far as the mind is stronger than the body, so are the ills contracted by the mind more severe than those contracted by the body.

It is our own evil thoughts which madden us.

If a man cannot feel the power of God when he looks upon the stars, then I doubt whether he is capable of any feeling at all.
