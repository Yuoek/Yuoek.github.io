Ben Jonson Quotes - Page 4

Many might go to heaven with half the labour they go to hell, if they would venture their industry the right way.

A prince without letters is a Pilot without eyes. All his government is groping.

Our whole life is like a play.

He that departs with his own honesty For Vulgar , doth it too dearly buy.

Princes that would their people should do well
Must at themselves begin, as at the head;
For men, by their example, pattern out
Their limitations, and regard of laws:
A virtuous court a world to virtue draws.

I do honor the very flea of his dog.

A good dog deserves a good bone.

Nor for my peace will I go far, As wanderers do, that still do roam, But make my strengths, such as they are, Here in my bosom, and at home.

He threatens many that hath injured one.

It strikes! one, two, Three, four, five, six. Enough, enough, dear watch, Thy pulse hath beat enough. Now sleep and rest; Would thou could'st make the time to do so too; I'll wind thee up no more.

Each petty hand
Can steer a ship becalm'd; but he that will
Govern and carry her to her ends, must know
His tides, his currents, how to shift his sails;
What she will bear in foul, what in fair weathers;
Where her springs are, her leaks, and how to stop 'em;
What strands, what shelves, what rocks do threaten her.

Hell itself must yield to industry.

Folly often goes beyond her bounds, but impudence knows none.

Cut Men's throats with whisperings.

A lily of a day
Is fairer far in May,
Although it fall and die that night,
It was the plant and flower of light.
In small proportions we just beauties see,
And in short measures life may perfect be.

The burnt child dreads the fire.

Blueness doth express trueness.

If you be sick, your own thoughts make you sick

I glory, more in the cunning purchase of my wealth than in the glad possession.

I perceive affection makes a fool Of any man too much the father.

All the wise world is little else, in nature, But parasites or subparasites.

The covetous man never has money. The prodigal will have none shortly.

I'll give anything for a good copy now, be it true or false, so it be news.

The man that is once hated, both his good and his evil deeds oppress him.

Let argument bear no unmusical sound.
