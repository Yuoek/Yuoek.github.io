Johann Wolfgang von Goethe Quotes - Page 10

Truth must be repeated again and again, because error is constantly being preached round about.

The flowers of life are but visionary. How many pass away and leave no trace behind! How few yield any fruit,--and the fruit itself, how rarely does it ripen! And yet there are flowers enough; and is it not strange, my friend, that we should suffer the little that does really ripen to rot, decay, and perish unenjoyed?

Life belongs to the living, and he who lives must be prepared for changes.

The man with insight enough to admit his limitations comes nearest to perfection.

This is the highest wisdom that I own; freedom and life are earned by those alone who conquer them each day anew.

Stupidity is without anxiety.

We are surrounded by abysses, but the greatest of all depths is in our own heart, and an irresistible leaning leads us there. Draw thyself from thyself!

They should be ashamed of themselves, all these sober people!

Doubt can only be removed by action.

Be generous with kindly words, especially about those who are absent.

The greatest joy of a thinking man is to have searched the explored and to quietly revere the unexplored.

Writing history is a method of getting rid of the past.

To rule is easy, to govern difficult.

Higher aims are in themselves more valuable, even if unfulfilled, than lower ones quite attained.

I am certain that I have been here as I am now a thousand times before, and I hope to return a thousand times... Man is a dialogue between nature and God. On other planets this dialogue will doubtless be of a higher and profounder character. What is lacking is Self-Knowledge. After that the rest will follow.

We must be young to do great things.

As long as you are not aware of the continual law of Die and Be Again, you are merely a vague guest on a dark Earth.

Science and art belong to the whole world, and before them vanish the barriers of nationality.

Riches amassed in haste will diminish; but those collected by hand and little by little will multiply.

Every reader, if he has a strong mind, reads himself into the book, and amalgamates his thoughts with those of the author.

How many years must a man do nothing, before he can at all know what is to be done and how to do it!

In the realm of ideas everything depends on enthusiasm... in the real world all rests on perseverance.

The really unhappy person is the one who leaves undone what they can do, and starts doing what they don't understand; no wonder they come to grief.

Love does not rule; but it trains, and that is more.

Money lost, something lost. Honor lost, much lost. Courage lost, everything lost-better you were never born
