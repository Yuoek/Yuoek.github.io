Some are trapped in boxes of pea-sized Christianity, full of myths about missions that rob them of incentive to care about the unreached

Prayer is action. By it we step out in advance of all other results . . . Praying is an activity upon which all others depend. By prayer we establish a beachhead for the kingdom among peoples where it has never been before. Prayer strikes the winning blow. All other missionary efforts simply gather up the fruits of our praying.

Pursuing prayer is prayer on a mission. It is diligent, fervent, constant, persevering, determined, and convinced.

It is just as proper, maybe even more so, to say Christ's global cause has a Church as to say Christ's Church has a global cause.

God cannot lead you on the basis of facts that you do not know.

Today five out of six non-Christians in our world have no hope unless missionaries come to them and plant the church among them.

Lack of world vision in any Christian produces a 'pea-sized Christianity'.

One is not old until Dreams become Regrets.

Leaders must be released from the idea that they must be great prayer warriors before they can begin to call others to prayer.

World Christians are heaven's expatriates, camping where the Kingdom is best served.
