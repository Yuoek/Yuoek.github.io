Ovid Quotes - Page 8

Lente, lente currite, noctis equi. Translation: Run slowly, slowly, horses of the night.

It is but a small merit to observe silence, but it is a grave fault to speak of matters on which we should be silent.

Spare the soul that feels a deadly wound.

Use the occasion, for it passes swiftly.

Live without envy, spend your peaceful years
Unknown to fame, and choose your peers for friends.

We always strive after what is forbidden, and desire the things refused us.

We two are to ourselves a crowd.

Wind feeds the fire, and wind extinguishes:
The flames are nourished by a gentle breeze,
Yet, if it stronger grows, they sink and die.

The burden becomes light that is shared by love.

If he did not succeed, he at least failed in a glorious undertaking.

A red rose peeping through a white? Or else a cherry (double graced) Within a lily? Centre placed? Or ever marked the pretty beam, A strawberry shows, half drowned in cream? Or seen rich rubies blushing through A pure smooth pearl, and orient too? So like to this, nay all the rest, Is each neat niplet of her breast.

Love is an affair of credulity.

The sharp thorn often produces delicate roses.

You can learn from anyone even your enemy.

Tempus fugit (time flies).

When I was from Cupid's passions free, my Muse was mute and wrote no elegy.

Time is a stream which glides smoothly on and is past before we know.

It is less to suffer punishment than to deserve it.
[Lat., Estque pati poenas quam meruisse minus.]

The spirited horse, which will try to win the race of its own accord, will run even faster if encouraged.

The result justifies the deed.

Note too that a faithful study of the liberal arts humanizes character and permits it not to be cruel.

Nothing is swifter than our years.

I could not possibly count the gold-digging ruses of women, Not if I had ten mouths, not if I had ten tongues.

That which never has been, never is, and never will be.

Heaven rewards the pious; those who cherish the gods
Themselves are cherished.
