Socrates Quotes - Page 7

Envy is the ulcer of the soul.

It is best and easiest not to discredit others but to prepare oneself to be as good as possible.

To fear death is nothing other than to think oneself wise when one is not. For it is to think one knows what one does not know. No one knows whether death may not even turn out to be the greatest blessings of human beings. And yet people fear it as if they knew for certain it is the greatest evil.

There is but one evil, ignorance.

He is a man of courage who does not run away, but remains at his post and fights against the enemy.

The noblest worship is to make yourself as good and as just as you can.

The poets are only the interpreters of the Gods.

Life without enquiry is not worth living for a man.

If one knows what is right, he will do it; nobody wants to be evil

It is better to be at odds with the whole world than, being one, to be at odds with myself.

The man who is truly wise knows that he knows very little.

The cure of many diseases remains unknown to the physicians of Hellos (Greece) because they do not study the whole person.

Wind buffs up empty bladders; opinion, fools.

Young people nowadays love luxury; they have bad manners and contempt for authority. They show disrespect for old people... contradict their parents, talk constantly in front of company, gobble their food and tyrannize their teachers.

I am very conscious that I am not wise at all.

Man's life is like a drop of dew on a leaf.

In all of us, even in good men, there is a lawless wild-beast nature, which peers out in sleep.

It is not living that matters, but living rightly.

I love to go and see all the things I am happy without.

Talk in order that I may see you.

Obscurity is dispelled by augmenting the light of discernment, not by attacking the darkness.

The misuse of language induces evil in the soul.

It is never right to do wrong or to requite wrong with wrong, or when we suffer evil to defend ourselves by doing evil in return.

To know, is to know that you know nothing. That is the meaning of true knowledge. For a man who claims to have knowledge, while actually knowing nothing, is less smarter than you, who claim to know nothing.

The best seasoning for food is hunger; for drink, thirst.
