Henry David Thoreau Quotes - Page 9

Make the most of your regrets; never smother your sorrow, but tend and cherish it till it comes to have a separate and integral interest. To regret deeply is to live afresh.

Trees indeed have hearts.

If a man does not keep pace with his companions, perhaps it is because he hears a different drummer. Let him step to the music which he hears, however measured or far away. It is not important that he should mature as soon as an apple-tree or oak. Shall he turn his spring into summer? If the condition of things which we were made for is not yet, what were any reality which we can substitute? We will not be shipwrecked on a vain reality.

Heroes are often the most ordinary of men.

It is not worth the while to let our imperfections disturb us always.

All this worldly wisdom was once the unamiable heresy of some wise man.

There is one consolation in being sick; and that is the possibility that you may recover to a better state than you were ever in before.

I know of no more encouraging fact than the unquestionable ability of man to elevate his life by conscious endeavor.

You must not blame me if I do talk to the clouds.

I have seen how the foundations of the world are laid, and I have not the least doubt that it will stand a good while.

I was not designed to be forced. I will breathe after my own fashion. Let us see who is the strongest.

We only need to be as true to others as we are to ourselves, that there may be grounds enough for friendship.

O how I laugh when I think of my vague indefinite riches. No run on my bank can drain it, for my wealth is not possession but enjoyment.

One man lies in his words, and gets a bad reputation; another in his manners, and enjoys a good one.

We need to witness our own limits transgressed, and some life pasturing freely where we never wander.

If the day and night are such that you greet them with joy, and life emits a fragrance like flowers and sweet-scented herbs, is more elastic, more starry, more immortal - that is your success.

I can alter my life by altering my attitude. He who would have nothing to do with thorns must never attempt to gather flowers.

Be resolutely and faithfully what you are; be humbly what you aspire to be.

We are always paid for our suspicion by finding what we suspect.

A book should contain pure discoveries, glimpses of terra firma, though by shipwrecked mariners, and not the art of navigation by those who have never been out of sight of land.

Who could believe in the prophecies ... that the world would end this summer, while one milkweed with faith matured its seeds.

If a man believes and expects great things of himself, it makes no odds where you put him, or what you show him . . he will be surrounded by grandeur.

Nature is full of genius, full of the divinity; so that not a snowflake escapes its fashioning hand.

I think that every town should have a park, or rather a primitive forest, of five hundred or a thousand acres, either in one body or several, where a stick would never be cut for fuel, not for the navy, not to make wagons, but stand and decay for higher uses - a common possession for instruction and recreation.

When I hear a grown man or woman say, "Once I had faith in men, now I have not," I am inclined to ask, "Who are you whom the world has disappointed? Have not you rather disappointed the world?"
