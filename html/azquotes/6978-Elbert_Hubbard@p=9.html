Elbert Hubbard Quotes - Page 9

Don't make excuses, make good.

War is the sure result of the existence of armed men. That country which maintains a large standing army will sooner or later have a war. The man who prides himself on fisticuffs is going, some day, to meet a man who considers himself the better man, and they will test the issue.

Prophecy - To observe that which has passed, and guess it will happen again.

Laughter is higher than all pain.

Character is the result of two things: mental attitude and the way we spend our time.

To supply a thought is mental massage; but to evolve a thought of your own is an achievement. Thinking is a brain exercise and no faculty grows save as it is exercised.

He who influences the thought of his times influences the times that follow.

What people need and what they want may be very different.... Teachers are those who educate the people to appreciate the things they need.

When trouble comes, wise men take to their work; weak men take to the woods.

Fortitude: That quality of mind which does not care what happens so long as it does not happen to us.

Meanness is more in half-doing than in omitting acts of generosity.

Theology, by diverting the attention of men from this life to another, and by endeavoring to coerce all men into one religion, constantly preaching that this world is full of misery, but the next world would be beautiful - or not, as the case may be - has forced on men the thought of fear where otherwise there might have been the happy abandon of nature.

If you don't advertise yourself you will be advertised by your loving enemies.

Life consists in molting our illusions. We form creeds today only to throw them away tomorrow. The eagle molts a feather because he is growing a better one.

Live truth instead of professing it.

The secret of success is this: there is no secret of success.

Make two grins grow where there was only a grouch before.

Logic: an instrument used for bolstering a prejudice.

Wealth is an engine that can be used for power, if you are an engineer; but to be tied to the fly wheel of an engine is rather a misfortune.

If you work for a man, in heavens name work for him! If he pays you wages that supply you your bread and butter, work for him speak well of him, think well of him, stand by him and stand by the institution he represents. I think if I worked for a man I would work for him. I would not work for him a part of the time, and the rest of the time work against him. I would give an undivided service or none. If put to the pinch, an ounce of loyalty is worth a pound of cleverness.

It may happen sometimes that a long debate becomes the cause of a longer friendship. Commonly, those who dispute with one another at last agree.

The great man is not so great as folks think, and the dull man is not quite so stupid as he seems. The difference in our estimates of men lies in the fact that one individual is able to get his goods into the show-window, and the other is not aware that he has any show-window or any goods.

Natural joy brings no headaches and no heartaches.

Where much is expected from an individual, he may rise to the level of events and make the dream come true.

Art is the beautiful way of doing things. Science is the effective way of doing things. Business is the economic way of doing things.
