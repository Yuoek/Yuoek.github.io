Meister Eckhart Quotes - Page 3

To the quiet mind all things are possible.

Compassion is where peace and justice kiss

To seek God by rituals is to get the ritual and lose God in the process

Our bodily food is changed into us, but our spiritual food changes us into it.

Whether you like it or not, whether you know it or not, secretly all nature seeks God and works toward [God].

There exists only the present instant ... There is no yesterday nor any tomorrow, but only Now.

A life of rest and peace in God is good; a life of pain lived in patience is still better; but to have peace in a life of pain is best of all.

You will have peace to the extent that you have God, and the further you are away from God the less you will be at peace... Thus you may measure your progress with God by measuring your peace or the lack of it.

Existence itself stands in need of nothing, for it lacks nothing, whereas everything else needs it, because outside of it there is nothing. Nothingness stands in need of existence, as a sick man lacks health and is in need. Health has no need of a sick man. To want nothing, therefore, characterizes the highest perfection, is fullest and purest existence.

People who have let go of themselves are so pure that the world cannot harm them.

Love is the root of all joy and sorrow.

The beast that bears you fastest to perfection is suffering.

God is like a person who clears his throat while hiding and so gives himself away. God lies in wait for us with nothing so much as love.

Nature's intent is neither food, nor drink, nor clothing, nor comfort, nor anything else in which God is left out. Whether you like it or not, whether you know it or not, secretly nature seeks, hunts, tries to ferret out the track on which God may be found.

We are all meant to be mothers of God, for God is always needing to be born.

Our best chance of finding God is to look in the place where we left him.

The outward man is the swinging door; the inner man is the still hinge.

I need to be silent for awhile, worlds are forming in my heart.

God is intelligence occupied with knowing itself.

Only those who have dared to let go can dare to re-enter.

The eye with which I see God is the same eye with which God sees me.

People should not worry so much about what they do but rather about what they are.

When the Soul wants to experience something she throws out an image in front of her and then steps into it.

I do not find God outside myself or conceive him except as my own and in me.

One must not always think so much about what one should do, but rather what one should be. Our works do not ennoble us; but we must ennoble our works.
