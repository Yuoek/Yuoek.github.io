Horace Quotes - Page 8

Wine brings to light the hidden secrets of the soul.

When a man is just and firm in his purpose,
The citizens burning to approve a wrong
Or the frowning looks of a tyrant
Do not shake his fixed mind, nor the Southwind.
Wild lord of the uneasy Adriatic,
Nor the thunder in the mighty hand of Jove:
Should the heavens crack and tumble down,
As the ruins crushed him he would not fear.

Pale Death beats equally at the poor man's gate and at the palaces of kings.

Being, be bold and venture to be wise.

Not to hope for things to last forever, is what the year teaches and even the hour which snatches a nice day away.

False praise can please, and calumny affright
None but the vicious, and the hypocrite.

Silver is less valuable than gold, gold than virtue.
[Lat., Vilius argentum est auro virtutibus aurum.]

Those who go overseas find a change of climate, not a change of soul.

It is no great art to say something briefly when, like Tacitus, one has something to say; when one has nothing to say, however, and none the less writes a whole book and makes truth into a liar - that I call an achievement.

It is the false shame of fools to try to conceal wounds that have not healed.

The good refrain from sin from the pure love of virtue.

The lofty pine is oftenest shaken by the winds; High towers fall with a heavier crash; And the lightning strikes the highest mountain.

There is a fault common to all singers. When they're among friends and are asked to sing they don't want to, and when they're not asked to sing they never stop.

The mob will now and then see things in a right light.

Whom does undeserved honour please, and undeserved blame alarm, but the base and the liar?

If things look badly to-day they may look better tomorrow.

The man who is just and resolute will not be moved from his settled purpose, either by the misdirected rage of his fellow citizens, or by the threats of an imperious tryant.

Envy is not to be conquered but by death.

Sad people dislike the happy, and the happy the sad; the quick thinking the sedate, and the careless the busy and industrious.

Punishment closely follows guilt as its companion.

Do you count your birthdays with gratitude?

A heart well prepared for adversity in bad times hopes, and in good times fears for a change in fortune.

Leave the rest to the gods.

It is your concern when your neighbor's wall is on fire.

Youth is unduly busy with pampering the outer person.
