Laozi Quotes - Page 3

To know and not to do is not yet to know.

Filling life exceedingly is called ominous.

If you never assume importance, you never lose it.

How can one liberate the many? By first liberating his own being. He does this not by elevating himself, but by lowering himself. He lowers himself to that which is simple, modest, true; integrating it into himself, he becomes a master of simplicity, modesty, truth.

To be learned, add something each day. To be enlightened drop something each day.

People aren't against you; they are for themselves. The most dangerous risk of all - the risk of spending your life not doing what you want on the bet you can buy yourself the freedom to do it later. He who conquers others is strong, he who conquers himself is mighty.

A tree that cannot bend will crack in the wind. Thus by nature's own decree, the soft and gentle are triumphant.

How do I become still?
By flowing with the stream.

To understand the limitation of things, desire them.

In the end, The treasure of life is missed by those who hold on and gained by those who let go

No weapon is sharper than will.

Hold on to the center and make up your mind to rejoice in this paradise called life.

The truth is not always beautiful, nor beautiful words the truth.

Throw away profit and greed, and there won't be any thieves.

All things of the world are born of being; being is born of nonbeing.

Being deeply loved by someone gives you strength, while loving someone deeply gives you courage.

Life is going forth; death is returning home.

Only fools seek power, and the greatest fools seek it through force.

Be empty. Be still. Just watch everything come and go. This is the way of Nature.

Those who know do not talk. Those who talk do not know.

Perfection is the willingness to be imperfect.

The high must make the low its base.

Chanting is no more holy than listening to the murmur of a stream, counting prayer beads no more sacred than simply breathing. . . . If you wish to attain oneness with the Tao, don't get caught up in spiritual superficialities.

See simplicity in the complicated. Achieve greatness in little things

Peacefulness is an inner sense of calm - it comes from becoming still - in order to reflect and meditate on our inner wisdom and receive answers. A peaceful heart is one that is free from worry and rouble. It's becoming quiet so we can look at things quietly so we can more clearly understand them and thus come up with creative solutions. It is learning to live in the present. Freedom from desire leads to inner peace.
