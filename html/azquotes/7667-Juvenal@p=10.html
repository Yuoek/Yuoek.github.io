Juvenal Quotes - Page 10

What day is so festal it fails to reveal some theft?

Like warmed-up cabbage served at each repast, The repetition kills the wretch at last.

One man meets an infamous punishment for that crime which confers a diadem on others.

When great assurance accompanies a bad undertaking, such is often mistaken for confiding sincerity by the world at large.

Savage bears agree with one another.

In their palate alone is their reason of existence.
[Lat., In solo vivendi causa palata est.]

Indignation leads to the making of poetry.
[Lat., Facit indignatio versum.]

To eat at another's table is your ambition's height.
[Lat., Bona summa putes, aliena vivere quadra.]

The Sicilian tyrants never devised a greater punishment than envy.

Let me moderate our sorrows. The grief of a man should not exceed proper bounds, but be in proportion to the blow he has received.
[Lat., Ponamus nimios gemitus: flagrantior aequo
Non debet dolor esse viri, nec vulnere major.]

Dare to do something worthy of transportation and a prison, if you mean to be anybody.

Do not pluck the beard of a dead lion.
[Lat., Noli
Barbam vellere mortuo leoni.]

The man whose purse is empty can cheerfully sing before the robber.

No nice extreme a true Italian knows;
But bid him go to hell, to hell he goes.

No one becomes depraved all at once.

The traveler without money will sing before the robber.
[Lat., Cantabit vacuus coram latrone viator.]

Those who do not wish to kill any one, wish they had the power.
[Lat., Et qui nolunt occidere quemquam
Posse volunt.]

For He, who gave this vast machine to roll, Breathed Life in then, in us a Reasoning Soul; That kindred feelings might our state improve, And mutual wants conduct to mutual love.

He who meditates a crime secretly within himself has all the guilt of the act.

Limits the Romans' anxieties to two things - bread and games.

All arts his own, the hungry Greekling counts; And bid him mount the skies, the skies he mounts.

Be a gentleman farmer.

Autumn is the harvest of greedy death.

But with what incessant and grievous ills is old age surrounded!

Fond man! though all the heroes of your line Bedeck your halls, and round your galleries shine In proud display; yet take this truth from me-- Virtue alone is true nobility!
