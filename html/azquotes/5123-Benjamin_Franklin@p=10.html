A wise man will desire no more than what he may get justly, use soberly, distribute cheerfully, and leave contently.

He that best understands the world, least likes it

If you want to make a friend, let someone do you a favor.

There are three faithful friends - an old wife, an old dog, and ready money.

There is scarce a king in a hundred who would not, if he could, follow the example of Pharoah - get first all the people's money, then all their lands, and then make them and their children servants forever.

Silence - Speak not but what may benefit others or yourself; avoid trifling conversation.

Without continual growth and progress, such words as improvement, achievement, and success have no meaning.

Be civil to all; serviceable to many; familiar with few; friend to one; enemy to none.

The Difficulty lies, in finding out an exact Measure but eat for Necessity, not Pleasure, for Lust knows not where Necessity ends.

Nothing is more fatal to health than an over care of it.

To be proud of knowledge is to be blind with light.

Time is money'... Waste it now. Pay for it later!

Wise men learn by others' harms, fools scarcely by their own.

Behold the rain which descends from heaven upon our vineyards; there it enters the roots of the vines, to be changed into wine; a constant proof that God loves us, and loves to see us happy.

Idle hands are the devil's playthings.

Where there's marriage without love, there will be love without marriage.

If you would know the value of money, go and try to borrow some.

If you want a thing done - go. If not - send.

Moderation in all things - including moderation.

Marriage is the most natural state of man, and therefore the state in which one is most likely to find solid happiness.

The strictest law sometimes becomes the severest injustice.

A Bible and a newspaper in every house, a good school in every district; all studied and appreciated as they merit; are the principal support of virtue, morality, and civil liberty.

But the most dangerous Hypocrite in a Common-Wealth, is one who leaves the Gospel for the sake of the Law: A Man compounded of Law and Gospel, is able to cheat a whole Country with his Religion, and then destroy them under Colour of Law: And here the Clergy are in great Danger of being deceiv'd, and the People of being deceiv'd by the Clergy, until the Monster arrives to such Power and Wealth, that he is out of the reach of both, and can oppress the People without their own blind Assistance.
